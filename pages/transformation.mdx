# Transformation

Transformation techniques in spectral analysis are applied to modify or enhance spectral data to improve its suitability for subsequent analysis or visualization. These techniques can involve mathematical operations, spectral manipulations, or combinations of both, aiming to extract meaningful information or facilitate data interpretation.

Transformations can serve various purposes, such as:

- **Intensity Corrections:** Adjusting the intensity values of spectra to compensate for instrumental variations, background signals, or scattering effects.
- **Spectral Transformations:** Converting spectra to different representations, like derivatives or Fourier transforms, to emphasize specific spectral features or reduce noise.
- **Data Reduction:** Simplifying spectral data by reducing its dimensionality or selecting specific spectral regions of interest.
- **Spectral Matching:** Aligning or comparing spectra acquired under different conditions or with varying resolutions.
